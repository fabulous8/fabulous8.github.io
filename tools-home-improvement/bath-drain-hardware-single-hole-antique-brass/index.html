DIY Installation Steps: you need plumber’s putty for installation First Step: Let’s remove your old drain. First put your old turn & lift drain in the down position. Twist to the left until the top piece comes off. Put a pair of plyers inside the drain. Use a wrench to grab the plyers and twist to the left. Keep going until the drain is out. You’ll find a rubber black washer under the tub where the drain was. Remove that and discard it. Completely clean up the drain with a wet rag and let it dry. Second Step: Let’s remove the top and insert washer. Just like last time, put the new turn & lift drain in the down position. Twist to the left until the top piece comes off. DON’T LOSE TOP PIECE Take the new, black rubber washer and slip it under the tub. You want this piece to be as straight as possible. Third Step: Take a medium sized ball of plumbers putty. Roll it on the tub until it is straight like a straw. Put it around the base of the drain, (above the bushing and the threading). Put the drain into the tub and fit it along the threading. Turn using the wrench and players like you used to take out the old drain. Just twist to the right this time. (A lot of the plumbers putty will come out and that’s perfect, just rub it off.) Twist the top piece that you removed earlier back in. That’s it! Should take 10-15 minutes to complete.
