WHITE CORN GRITS:- Corn Grits are a popular dry corn product because of its long shelf life. It is used to produce an assortment of chemically leavened bread and fried products, and is especially popular as a nutritious hot cereal or a side dish. OLD FASHION GRITS:- These are the old fashion type grits, not the instant grits. There are no additives in them. They haven’t been pre-cooked or fortified in any way. It is just natural corn. True grits like these are literally nothing more than plain white corn that has been ground down to a coarse sand-type texture after most of the husk and germ has been removed. PREPARATION:- Bring water to boil. Whisk the grits into the water. Simmer for 20-30 minutes. Use 4 cups of water to 1 cup of grits. Add more water if you prefer your grits “soupy.” Season to taste (salt, pepper, butter, or even cheese).
