MAINTENCE - The MARK 5 Die-Cutting Machine and retired MARK IV Machine require periodic replacement of the roller and die tray. These items last about one year with average use. TIME TO REPLACE - With heavy use, they may need to be replaced more often. You will notice that the new roller is black. The roller wears down with use and needs to be replaced when the roller turns white and cutting performance is diminished. HOW TO EXTEND LIFE - Rolling a die through while it is on top of scrap material, or purposely shimming under a die, causes the blades to push through back of die. COMPATABILITY - AccuCut MARK 5 and MARK IV Die Cutting Machine INCLUDED – One AccuCut MARK 5 and MARK IV Roller

How to Use: Step 1 Facing the back of the machine, remove the roller safety guard and set aside. The MARK 5 has a steel safety guard, and the MARK IV has a plastic safety guard. Step 2 Still facing the back of the machine (the side WITHOUT the handle), remove the two screws then take off and set aside the cover plate. The MARK 5 Machine has easy-to-remove screws that are removable by hand. Step 3 Pull out the roller and discard it. You will know it’s time to change the roller when it turns white or gray. Step 4 Find the end of the roller with the open channel and slide that end into the machine first. Rotate roller until it locks into place and the handle turns with the roller. Step 5 Replace cover plate, screws and safety guard. Warning: Safety guard must be in place during operation. Keep hands and fingers clear of roller or serious bodily injury can occur.
